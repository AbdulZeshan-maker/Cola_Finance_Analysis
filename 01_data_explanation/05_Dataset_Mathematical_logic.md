#### **Mathematical Explanation of all sheets**





##### **Profit and Loss Statement Sheet:**



**Profit and Loss (P\&L) statement works like a waterfall: it starts with the total revenue brought in at the top and sequentially deducts costs and expenses (while adding any secondary income) to arrive at the final bottom-line profit.**



**Here is the exact mathematical logic for FY '09, moving row by row:**



**1. Calculating Gross Profit**

**This step determines how much money the company made after factoring in the direct costs of producing its goods.**



**Formula: Net Operating Revenues - Cost of goods sold**



**Calculation: 30990 - 11088 = 19902**



**Result: The Gross Profit is 19902.**



**2. Calculating Operating Income**

**Next, we deduct the indirect operational costs (like salaries, rent, and marketing) from the Gross Profit to see how much money the core business operations generated.**



**Formula: Gross Profit - Selling, general and administrative expenses - Other operating charges**



**Calculation: 19902 - 11358 - 313 = 8231**



**Result: The Operating Income is 8231.**



**3. Calculating Income Before Income Taxes**

**A company often has financial activities outside its core operations, such as earning interest on cash reserves, paying interest on debt, or earning money from investments. We adjust the Operating Income with these figures.**



**Formula: Operating Income + Interest income - Interest expense + Equity income (loss) + Other income (loss)**



**Calculation: 8231 + 249 - 355 + 781 + 40 = 8946**



**Result: The Income before income taxes is 8946.**



**4. Calculating Consolidated Net Income**

**Now, we must pay the government. We deduct the income tax expense from our pre-tax income.**



**Formula: Income before income taxes - Income taxes**



**Calculation: 8946 - 2040 = 6906**



**Result: The Consolidated Net Income is 6906.**



**Note on the "Net income from continuing operations" row: This figure is also 6906 because the company had 0 discontinued operations in FY '09. All of the consolidated net income came from their ongoing, continuing operations.**



**5. Calculating Net Income Attributable to Shareowners**

**Finally, "Consolidated" means the statement might include profits from subsidiaries that the parent company doesn't own 100%. We have to subtract the portion of the profit that belongs to outside investors (noncontrolling interests) to find out exactly what belongs to the parent company's shareholders.**



**Formula: Consolidated Net Income - Net income attributable to noncontrolling interests + Income (Loss) from Discontinued Operations**



**Calculation: 6906 - 82 + 0 = 6824**



**Result: The final NET INCOME ATTRIBUTABLE TO SHAREOWNERS is 6824.**



**This final number (6824) is the true bottom line for the shareholders of the company for FY '09. The identical mathematical logic applies to every other fiscal year column in the sheet.**





##### **Balance Sheet Statement:**



**A Balance Sheet is a financial snapshot that follows the fundamental accounting equation: Total Assets = Total Liabilities + Total Equity. It shows what a company owns (Assets), what it owes (Liabilities), and the residual value belonging to owners (Equity).**



**Here is the exact mathematical logic for FY '09, grouped by the major calculated sub-totals on the sheet:**



**1. Calculating Total Cash**

**First, the statement groups the most liquid assets. This is simply the sum of actual cash on hand and highly liquid short-term investments.**



**Cash and cash equivalents: 7021**



**Short-term investments: 2130**



**Formula: Cash and cash equivalents + Short-term investments**



**Calculation: 7021 + 2130 = 9151**



**Result: Total cash (Cash and cash equivalents + Short-term investment) is 9151.**



**2. Calculating Total Current Assets**

**Current assets are assets the company expects to convert to cash or use up within one year. We take the Total Cash calculated above and add the rest of the short-term assets.**



**Total cash: 9151**



**Marketable securities: 62**



**Trade accounts receivable, less allowances: 3758**



**Inventories: 2354**



**Prepaid expenses and other assets: 2226**



**Assets held for sale\_1: 0**



**Assets held for sale\_2: 0**



**Formula: Sum of all current asset line items**



**Calculation: 9151 + 62 + 3758 + 2354 + 2226 + 0 + 0 = 17551**



**Result: Total current assets are 17551.**



**3. Calculating Total Assets**

**This figure represents everything the company owns. We add the long-term (non-current) assets—such as property, equipment, and intangible assets like trademarks—to our previously calculated Total Current Assets.**



**Total current assets: 17551**



**Equity method investments: 6217**



**Other investments: 538**



**Other assets: 1976**



**Property, plant and equipment - net: 9561**



**Deferred income tax assets: 0**



**TRADEMARKS WITH INDEFINITE LIVES: 6183**



**BOTTLERS' FRANCHISE RIGHTS WITH INDEFINITE LIVES: 1953**



**Goodwill: 4224**



**Other intangible assets: 468**



**Formula: Total current assets + Sum of all non-current asset line items**



**Calculation: 17551 + 6217 + 538 + 1976 + 9561 + 0 + 6183 + 1953 + 4224 + 468 = 48671**



**Result: Total assets are 48671.**



**4. Calculating Total Current Liabilities**

**Now we move to the obligations side of the sheet. Current liabilities are debts and obligations the company must pay within one year.**



**Accounts payable and accrued expenses: 6657**



**Loans and notes payable: 6749**



**Current maturities of long-term debt: 51**



**Accrued income taxes: 264**



**Liabilities held for sale: 0**



**Liabilities Held for Sale, Discontinued Operations: 0**



**Formula: Sum of all short-term liability line items**



**Calculation: 6657 + 6749 + 51 + 264 + 0 + 0 = 13721**



**Result: Total current liabilities are 13721.**



**5. Calculating Total Equity**

**Equity represents the shareholders' stake in the company. To find the total equity, we first calculate the equity specifically belonging to the parent company's shareholders, then add any equity belonging to minority investors (noncontrolling interests).**



**Common stock: 880**



**Capital surplus: 8537**



**Reinvested earnings: 41537**



**Accumulated other comprehensive income (loss): -757 (Subtracted because it's a loss)**



**Treasury stock, at cost: -25398 (Subtracted because these are shares the company bought back, reducing equity)**



**Calculation for Shareowner Equity: 880 + 8537 + 41537 - 757 - 25398 = 24799 (EQUITY ATTRIBUTABLE TO SHAREOWNERS OF THE COCA-COLA COMPANY)**



**Adding Noncontrolling interests: 24799 + 547 = 25346**



**Result: Total equity is 25346.**



**6. The Final Balancing Calculation**

**To prove the balance sheet is accurate, the sum of all Liabilities and Equity must exactly match the Total Assets calculated in Step 3. We take the Total Current Liabilities, add the long-term liabilities, and finally add the Total Equity.**



**Total current liabilities: 13721**



**Long-term debt: 5059**



**Other liabilities: 2965**



**Deferred income taxes: 1580**



**Total equity: 25346**



**Formula: Total current liabilities + Long-term debt + Other liabilities + Deferred income taxes + Total equity**



**Calculation: 13721 + 5059 + 2965 + 1580 + 25346 = 48671**



**Result: Total liabilities and equity are 48671.**



###### **As you can see, the Total Liabilities and Equity (48671) perfectly matches the Total Assets (48671), which is why it is called a "balance" sheet.**





##### **Cash Flow Statement:**



**A Cash Flow Statement reconciles the company's net income to the actual cash that moved in and out of the business during a given period. It tracks cash across three main areas: Operating Activities, Investing Activities, and Financing Activities. Positive numbers represent cash coming into the company, and negative numbers represent cash going out.**



**Here is the exact mathematical logic for every line item in FY '09:**



**1. Net Cash Provided by Operating Activities**

**This section starts with the company's net income and adjusts it for non-cash expenses (like depreciation) and changes in working capital to show exactly how much actual cash was generated by day-to-day operations.**



**Note: CONSOLIDATED NET INCOME (6906) matches "Net income from continuing operations" (6906) because there is a 0 value for (Income) Loss from discontinued operations.**



**Calculation:**



**Net income from continuing operations: 6906**



**(Add) Depreciation and amortization: 1236**



**(Add) Stock-based compensation expense: 241**



**(Add) Deferred income taxes: 353**



**(Subtract) Equity (income) loss - net of dividends: -359**



**(Add) Foreign currency adjustments: 61**



**(Subtract) Significant (gains) losses on sales of assets - net: -43**



**(Add) Other significant (gains) losses - net: 0**



**(Add) Other operating charges: 134**



**(Add) Other items: 221**



**(Subtract) Net change in operating assets and liabilities: -564**



**Formula: 6906 + 1236 + 241 + 353 - 359 + 61 - 43 + 0 + 134 + 221 - 564**

**Result: 8186 (Net cash provided by operating activities)**



**2. Net Cash Provided by (Used in) Investing Activities**

**This section tracks cash spent on or generated from long-term investments, such as buying/selling property, equipment, or other businesses.**



**Calculation:**



**(Subtract) Purchases of short-term investments: -2130**



**(Subtract) Purchases of investments: -22**



**(Add) Proceeds from disposals of investments: 0**



**(Subtract) Acquisitions of businesses, equity method investments...: -300**



**(Add) Proceeds from disposals of businesses, equity method investments...: 240**



**(Subtract) Purchases of property, plant and equipment: -1993**



**(Add) Proceeds from disposals of property, plant and equipment: 104**



**(Subtract) Other investing activities: -48**



**Formula: -2130 - 22 + 0 - 300 + 240 - 1993 + 104 - 48**

**Result: -4149 (Net cash provided by (used in) investing activities)**



**3. Net Cash Provided by (Used in) Financing Activities**

**This section tracks the cash flow between the company and its owners or creditors, such as issuing/paying debt, paying dividends, or repurchasing stock.**



**Calculation:**



**(Add) Issuances of debt: 14689**



**(Subtract) Payments of debt: -12326**



**(Add) Issuances of stock: 664**



**(Subtract) Purchases of stock for treasury: -1518**



**(Subtract) Dividends: -3800**



**(Subtract) Other financing activities: -2**



**Formula: 14689 - 12326 + 664 - 1518 - 3800 - 2**

**Result: -2293 (Net cash provided by (used in) financing activities)**



**4. Final Net Increase (Decrease) During the Year**

**To find the final change in cash for the year, we sum up the totals from the three main activities above, and add any adjustments for exchange rates or discontinued operations.**



**Calculation:**



**Net cash provided by operating activities: 8186**



**Net cash provided by (used in) investing activities: -4149**



**Net cash provided by (used in) financing activities: -2293**



**Cash Provided by (Used in) Operating Activities, Discontinued Operations: 0**



**Effect of exchange rate changes on cash and cash equivalents: 576**



**Cash Provided by (Used in) Investing Activities, Discontinued Operations: 0**



**Cash Provided by (Used in) Financing Activities, Discontinued Operations: 0**



**Net Cash Provided by (Used in) Discontinued Operations: 0**



**Formula: 8186 - 4149 - 2293 + 0 + 576 + 0 + 0 + 0**

**Result: 2320 (Net increase during the year)**



**By adding the total cash movements from operations, investments, financing, and exchange rate effects, the statement shows that the company increased its cash position by exactly 2320 over the course of FY '09.**
