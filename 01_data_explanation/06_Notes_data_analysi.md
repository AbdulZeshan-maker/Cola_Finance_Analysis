###### **Points to remember for data analysis**





1\. The **P\&L** proves your business model works. The Cash Flow statement does all the adding and subtracting necessary to prove you actually have physical money to spend.

2\. But remember, the Cash Flow Statement always starts with the final number from the P\&L

In **cash flow statements** The Rule of Isolation works

When you look at a Cash Flow Statement, Operating Activities has a very strict rule: It is only allowed to show the cash generated by the business that is still alive, if the dead business is mixed into that starting number, we have to mathematically pull it out. We do this not to pretend the cash wasn't lost, but to move that loss to a different part of the page.

3\. If cash was deducted in profit and loss statement , but no cash was paid then in cash flow statement that money will be added to the net income, because cash flow statement shows the real cash flow, similarly if there was an equity income-paper profit, but no cash received then that will be subtracted from the net income, because in profit and loss statement it was already added, and profit loss statement just gives us an idea about the growth of the company, whereas cash flow statement only updates if there is real cash flow, like you may be rich on paper, but if you don't have real cash then you might not be considered poor.

4 .A **Balance Sheet** is a financial snapshot that follows the fundamental accounting equation: Total Assets = Total Liabilities + Total Equity
